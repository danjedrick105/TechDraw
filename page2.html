DIY STYLUS PEN

A homemade stylus pen is a Do-It-Yourself gadget that is made to be used with touchscreen screens. It usually consists of a conductive medium, such as aluminum foil, that replicates the electric characteristics of a human finger. The minimum form usually comprises a pen or a pencil as the core, and aluminum foil wrapped around it for the purpose of conducting electricity. For extra comfort, a sponge or cotton swab can be applied at the tip to provide a softer and more accurate contact point. The end result is a working and cost-effective substitute for commercially available styluses!

Steps:

1. Prepare the Pen or Pencil:

Use an old pen or pencil. If using a pen, remove the ink cartridge to reduce weight.

2. Wrap with Aluminum Foil:

Wrap foil tightly around the pen, leaving about an inch exposed at the tip.

3. Make the Tip:

For a softer tip, attach a sponge or cotton swab at the writing end and secure it with foil.

4. Secure with Tape:

Use tape to hold the foil firmly in place.

5. Test the Stylus:

Try using your stylus on a touchscreen; it should work just like a store-bought one!

Materials Needed:

Pen/Pencil, Aluminum foil, Tape, Sponge/Cotton Swab, Scissors

Body Components

Drawing body parts requires subdividing the human form into simple forms and shapes such as circles, ovals, and cylinders to plan the head, torso, arms, legs, and joints. Based on these simplified forms, you enhance the muscles, contours, and finer details without sacrificing proportion and symmetry. The inclusion of movement and gesture adds vitality to the sketch, accentuating the body's natural flow and movement.

Human Head

Drawing a human head is done by making an oval shape with a top and chin. Vertical and horizontal guidelines split the head into equal halves and eye level. Place facial features such as eyes, nose, mouth, and jawline with horizontal lines. This technique guarantees symmetry, balance, and proportion in a human face drawing.

Steps:

1. Draw the Basic Shape

Start with a light pencil and sketch an oval or an egg-like shape for the head. The narrower end will be the chin, and the wider end will represent the top of the head.

2. Add Guidelines

Lightly draw a vertical line down the center of the oval to divide the head into two equal halves. Draw a horizontal line across the middle to mark the eye level. For a front view, this line divides the oval into approximately two equal parts.

3. Mark Facial Features

Eyes: Divide the horizontal line into five equal sections. The eyes will sit on the second and fourth sections, with one section space between them.

Nose: Draw a horizontal guideline halfway between the eye line and the chin for the base of the nose.

Mouth: Place another guideline about halfway between the nose and the chin for the mouth. Adjust the width of the mouth to align with the center of the eyes.

4. Draw the Jawline

Define the jawline by adding lines from the sides of the oval down to the chin. Make it smooth and symmetrical.
